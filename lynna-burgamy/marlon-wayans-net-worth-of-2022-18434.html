Marlon Wayans was raised in a housing project in New York City

Marlon Wayans, who was born on July 23, 1972, is a member of the Wayans family’s first generation of entertainers. He is the tenth child of Howell Stouten Wayans, a supermarket manager, and Elvira Alethia (Green), a homemaker and social worker.

Marlon and his siblings grew up in a New York City housing project. He attended Howard University after earning his diploma from the renowned Fiorello H. LaGuardia High School of Music, Art, and Performing Arts.

Marlon made his debut with the Wayans clan

Between 1992 and 1993, Marlon made appearances on In Living Color alongside his older siblings Damon, Keenen, Kim, and Shawn.

Along with the Wayans family, Jim Carrey, David Alan Grier, Kim Coles, and Jamie Foxx all got their start in acting thanks to the popular sitcom. Jennifer Lopez was a Fly Girl in it as well.

Marlon and Shawn Wayans co-starred in the enduring WB sitcom The Wayans Bros. from 1995 to 1999.

He was hired to play the superhero sidekick Robin around that period for DC’s Batman Returns and Batman Forever. But when Joel Schumacher took over as director of the latter movie, Chris O’Donnell was cast in the part.

Billy Dee Williams, who played Two-Face, and Marlon both accepted payouts and continue to collect royalties to this day.

The Scary Movie series, which Marlon and his brother Shawn produced, is his main source of popularity. He has additionally acted in blockbusters including White Chicks, G.I. Joe: The Rise of Cobra, and Naked.

He received his own sitcom, Marlon, from NBC in 2017. Before Netflix obtained the sole distribution rights, Marlon ran for two seasons on NBC.

Additionally, he signed a stand-up contract with Netflix, where his latest film, Sextuplets, will debut in August 2019.

The God of Parody Films is Marlon

Blockbuster spoof movies are the brainchild of Marlon and Shawn Wayans. They both contribute to the scripts and co-star in the films; Marlon has earned $457.0 million at the global box office.

The parody films Scary Movie from 2000 and its follow-up from 2001 are cult favorites. After the first two films, which have a combined lifetime gross of $157.0 million and $71.0 million, The Wayans quit the franchise.

In 2009, Damien Dante Wayans made his feature film debut with the satire Dance Flick. Several Wayans family members, including Marlon, contributed to the script for the film.

In A Haunted House and its follow-up, Marlon returned to the horror-comedy genre by mocking found-footage films. A year after the first film’s debut in 2016, he wasted no time in parodying Fifty Shades of Grey in the comedy film Fifty Shades of Black.
