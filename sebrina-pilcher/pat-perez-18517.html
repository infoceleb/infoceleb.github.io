Career

On January 22, 2009, Perez finished the first 36 holes of the Bob Hope Classic with 124 (−20), the lowest score in the history of the PGA Tour in two rounds. The start set or tied several records, including a low score record (124) in consecutive rounds. Perez went on to win the tournament by three strokes over John Merrick, a win that was secured when Perez hit his second shot in the par 5, 18th hole, from 200 yards to 3 feet to win the final eagle for his first-round win.
