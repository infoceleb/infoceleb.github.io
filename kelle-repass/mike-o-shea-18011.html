Biography

Mike O’Shea was born in Canada on September 21, 1970. Football coach who became the head coach of the Canadian Football League’s Winnipeg Blue Bombers in 2014 after spending four seasons as the special teams coordinator for the Toronto Argonauts. He is also one of the most revered defensive players in CFL history, having won three Grey Cups with the Argonauts during his career. He won two Grey Cups alongside future NFL quarterback Doug Flutie. He was picked by the Edmonton Eskimos with the fourth overall pick in the 1993 CFL Canadian College Draft.
