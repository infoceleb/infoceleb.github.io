Biography

Cy Twombly was born in Virginia on April 25, 1928. Known for his graffiti-style paintings and large-scale works such as “Fifty Days at Iliam” and “The Four Seasons.” His work was acquired for the permanent collections of Paris’ Musée du Louvre and New York’s Museum of Modern Art. He and his father were both named after baseball legend Cy Young. He was a student of Spanish artist Pierre Daura when he was 12. He attended Virginia’s Washington and Lee University and the School of the Museum of Fine Arts in Boston.
