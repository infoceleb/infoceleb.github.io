Biography

Suzanne Vega was born in Santa Monica, CA on July 11, 1959. Award-winning singer-songwriter whose folk-inspired songs “Luka” and “Tom’s Diner” both became international top ten hits. Part of her song “Tom’s Diner” was used in the song “Dopefiend’s Diner” by Tupac Shakur. She studied modern dance at New York’s High School of Performing Arts. While at Barnard College, she began performing in Greenwich Village.
