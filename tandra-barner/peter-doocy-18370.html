Peter Doocy- Biography

Peter Doocy is an American journalist who is best known for serving as the Fox News Channel’s White House Correspondent. He is also the son of Steve Doocy, a well-known Fox, and Friends co-host.

Peter Doocy was born on July 21, 1987, in Washington, DC, to his parents, Steve Doocy and Kathy Gerrity. Peter Doocy- Professional Career

Peter began his career as an intern for a variety of channels and events, including Fox. After graduating in 2009, he went to work for Fox News in New York City as a general assignment reporter. He covered a variety of key events, including natural disasters, presidential campaigns, and so on. Peter has covered a variety of stories, including former Vice President Dick Cheney’s heart surgery in 2012, the Midwest floods in 2011, Hurricane Sandy, and the presidential elections in 2008 and 2012. He also covered the shooting at Sandy Hook Elementary School.

In 2014, Peter rose to prominence after conducting an exclusive interview with Robert O’Neill, the guy who claimed to have shot Osama Bin Laden. It was included in a Fox Channel documentary, which went on to become the channel’s highest-rated documentary.

He also covered Joe Biden’s presidential bid for the Democratic Party in 2020. In January 2021, Doocy was hired as a White House Correspondent for Fox Channel and assigned to cover Biden’s presidential campaign. He is currently based in Washington, DC, as a correspondent for “Capitol Hill” and a White House Correspondent.
